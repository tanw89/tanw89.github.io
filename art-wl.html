Sketchy Details

How Art Thou

I first started sketching on a more regular basis in 2013. Sketching allowed me to take in sights more intentionally, and to truly appreciate the moment instead of clamouring to capture it on camera, which sometimes “dilutes” the experience and memory.

I hope to be able to sketch better, and when time permits, try out other media such as acrylic or watercolour. It's just hard, because time keeps slipping away, like in that Salvador Dali painting. Hmm.

Inequality, A Diptych
2017

×


As a sociology student, I have always been very intrigued by inequality. This diptych was birthed out of that interest.

The idea for this came about when I stepped out of Bugis Plus one evening in 2017 to see a large rainbow. I was aware of the sparkling, glitzy mall behind me, MBS in the background, and the hawker centre in front of me where some elderly men were chatting over beer.

I couldn't help but wonder how each person saw the same rainbow.

SchemesSG, a mobile
assistance directory

×
SchemesSG is a mobile app built with React Native. It is a directory of various help schemes available in Singapore, inclusive of both government schemes as well as those by private bodies or NGOs.

Although many government apps already exist to provide info on assistance, I felt that there was a gap in information regarding schemes disbursed at the private or local level, say by charitable organisations, individuals or even Members of Parliament. Hence I built a proof-of-concept directory as follows:




The app also sports a simple "Add to Favourites" function made with Redux. This allows the user to go back to saved schemes for further follow-up.




I am exploring the possibility of integrating the app with a database which can be updated regularly with new scheme listings and information. Currently I am toying around with Firebase, but if you know of a better solution, please reach out to me!

The UX Diaries - A UX
review journal

×
The UX Diaries is a journal where I record my experience and reflections of using government apps. It also details how I, as a user, would like to see these apps evolve.

Built using ReactJS, I have recorded my thoughts of about 12 apps:


In the journal, I address 3 key areas for each app: 1) what I think users want to do through the app, 2) what the organisation is trying to achieve, and 3) how behavioural science can inform the roadmapping of features. These thought experiments help me establish design sensibilities and become more acute of my own needs as a user.


While I could have used a ready-made site editor, building this journal from scratch also helped me appreciate the complexities that go behind planning even a simple page. Mad kudos to dedicated React developers!

I'll be publishing the site soon once I create more reviews and thought experiments!
